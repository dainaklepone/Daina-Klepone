ABOUT

THE SHADOW BEHIND THE STORIES

Daina Klepone

Crime Thriller Author

I've always been drawn to the shadows. As a child growing up in a small coastal town, I spent countless hours reading noir classics and crime fiction, fascinated by the moral ambiguity and psychological depth of characters who walked the line between light and darkness.

Before becoming a full-time author, I worked as a criminal psychologist, spending fifteen years interviewing and analyzing the minds of those society labels as monsters. This experience gave me unique insights into the darker corners of human psychology—insights that now fuel my fiction.

My Morgan Chase series follows a detective haunted by his own past as he navigates a city where corruption and crime lurk beneath a veneer of respectability. Like all my protagonists, Morgan is flawed, complex, and constantly questioning the thin line between justice and vengeance.

When I'm not writing, you'll find me exploring abandoned urban locations, collecting vintage crime memorabilia, or simply sitting in dimly lit coffee shops observing the countless untold stories that pass by.

I believe the best noir fiction doesn't just entertain—it challenges our perceptions of morality and forces us to confront uncomfortable truths about human nature. That's what I strive for in every book I write.

MY WRITING PROCESS

RESEARCH

Every story begins with extensive research. I interview detectives, visit crime scenes, and immerse myself in the psychological profiles of both victims and perpetrators. Authenticity is crucial in noir fiction.

CHARACTERS

My characters are born from the shadows of real people I've encountered. I spend months developing detailed backstories and psychological profiles before writing a single scene.

SETTING

In noir fiction, the setting is as important as any character. I create atmospheric, morally ambiguous worlds where the line between right and wrong blurs in the shadows.

WRITING

I write at night, when the world is quiet and the shadows are longest. First drafts are raw and unfiltered; editing is where the real craft emerges, polishing the darkness until it gleams.

INFLUENCES

My writing has been shaped by the masters of noir and crime fiction, as well as my own experiences walking the thin line between light and shadow.
